Fatos sobre o Stomatopoda

Classification

Odontodactylus scyllarus

Kingdom Animalia
Phylum Arthropoda
Subphylum Crustacea
Class Malacostraca
Subclass Hoplocarida
Order Stomatopoda; Latreille, 1817

Description

Stomatopods are active predators that hunt prey with the aid of a very keen sense of sight. accurate and capable of interpreting the polarization in the ultraviolet spectrum and infrared. They present a great variation in size, which can range from a few millimeters to approximately 40 cm in larger species. they live in background consolidated, muddy or even sandy, where they dig their holes or take advantage of the holes left by other animals to settle in them. They are exclusively carnivorous animals, feeding on shrimps, crabs, molluscs, fish and even others of the same order. the second pair of legs, very developed, is used both to attack the prey and to defend itself. the uropod, when open, it also works for defense, like a shield, closing the gallery the animal is in installed. The female spawns in the place where she shelters and, in case of danger, rolls her eggs into a ball, trapping them close to the body until they find a more protected shelter.

Capacity

The biggest crushers, such as specimens of Odontodactylus scyllarus, are able to deliver one of the fastest, most vicious blows in the animal kingdom, a punch that can present the .22 caliber shot (equivalent to 720 km/h) and an impact pressure of 600 N/cm². This overwhelming strength is responsible for her title of "lobster boxer" and is capable of easily cracking a crab's carapace, the hard, calcified shells of gastropods or even break the reinforced glass of an aquarium.
